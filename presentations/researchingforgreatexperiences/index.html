What is UX Really?

UX relies on informed facts* as opposed to assumptions

User Research

Testing

Research = discovery of hypothesis

Research

Qualitative

Not asking people what they want

Nor selling or convincing them of an idea...

Questions we try to answer with research

  • Who are our users? (Personas)
  • What is it you are trying to accomplish? Why?
  • How are you accomplishing X today?
  • What are any issues you have when accomplishing X now?
  • What are some benefits to how you are accomplishing X now?

Research Examples

  • Surveys
  • Interviews (in person, skype, phone)
  • Shadowing
  • Contextual Inquiry
  • Card sorting
  • Co-Designing

Interview Goals

Remove any assumptions, gather more facts and evidence for informing decisions.

Gain a deeper understanding of their needs.

Interview Process

  • One on one (groupthink is misinforming)
  • 10-30 minutes
  • without a computer
  • Open ended, non leading questions (Why? How? What is easy about this? What is difficult about this?)
  • Without proposing final solutions ie. "Would you like this idea?""

Results

  • Findings must be shared with the team, not just decisions
  • Who are users, their goals, their workflows.
  • Deep understanding of design direction from multiple users perspective
  • Hypothesis for designs

Example

Approach 10 people with no prior knowledge of the company. Discuss goals, current steps and identify pain points surrounding a landing page generator used for corporate events.

Research = discovery of hypothesis

Testing = validation of hypothesis

Testing

Qualitative and quantitative

Not asking people what they want

Nor selling or convincing them of an idea...

Questions we try to answer with testing

  • Do designs support our hypothesis identified during research?
  • While trying to solve for X, does Y allow users to accomplish a task? Why and why not?

Testing Examples

  • Informal usability testing aka Guerrilla Usability
  • Formal Usability
  • A/B
  • Analytics
  • Tracking/heat mapping

Informal Usability Goals

Gather objective feedback on designs to aid decision making

Gaining a deeper understanding of their needs.

Informal Usability Process

  • One on one (groupthink is misinforming)
  • coffee shops, skype, phone
  • 15-45 minutes
  • with a tangible (any and all of the following apply: sketches, wireframes, hi fidelity mockups, clickable prototype, fully functioning software)
  • set scenarios within the context of a story for accomplishing tasks (tasks discovered from research)

Results

  • Findings must be shared with the team, not just decisions
  • What else did we learn about our users?
  • How did our hypothesis fail? How did our hypothesis succeed?
  • How can we improve?
  • Refine hypothesis
  • Refine designs

Example

Running through three scenarios including onboarding, and two use cases for a location based mobile app. Tangible was balsamiq mockups in a clickable format on an iphone.
